A recurring observation from the past six months of using generative tools in production work: AI fails at precisely the moments where a designer would say "it just felt wrong."

This is philosophically interesting. It means our highest-value judgment is *pre-linguistic* — it operates below the level of articulable criteria.

## What the signal points to

If you can fully specify the criteria for good design output, an AI can approximate it. The gap is always in the unspecified criteria — the ones that make the work feel *authored* rather than *produced*.

This suggests the most defensible position for a design practice is not technical sophistication but a cultivated sensitivity that *can't be fully documented*.

The paradox: the less you can explain it, the harder it is to replace.

## Calibration

The studios that will feel this least are the ones already operating at the level of conceptual authorship — where the design is the least important part of what they deliver, and the thinking is the actual product.
